## What Is Holistic Life Coaching?
Holistic Life Coaching is goal-oriented, values-driven work that helps you get clear on who you are, what you truly want, and what has been standing between you and it. Unlike traditional coaching (which focuses primarily on external strategy), holistic coaching works at the level of mindset, belief, and inner alignment — because lasting external change always starts inside.

Fern Wolf trained under Alan Cohen, founder of the Holistic Life Coaching Foundation.

## Who Is This For?
Life coaching is a fit if you're:

- At a crossroads and don't know which path to take
- Achieving things that still don't feel fulfilling
- Stuck in a pattern of relationships, work, or habits that isn't serving you
- Carrying grief, trauma, or unresolved emotion that affects your daily life
- Ready to set goals that actually matter — and learn how to reach them
- Wanting to build confidence, set stronger boundaries, or find your voice

## What to Expect in Sessions
1. An honest conversation about where you are, where you want to be, and what's in the way
2. Identifying the beliefs, fears, or patterns that have been keeping you stuck
3. Practical tools, perspectives, and strategies for moving forward
4. Accountability and support session to session

## FAQ

**Q: How is coaching different from therapy?**
A: Coaching is forward-focused — it's about building your future, not processing your past. While it may touch on past patterns or emotions, the primary orientation is where you're going and how to get there.

**Q: How often do we meet?**
A: That's entirely up to you. There's no set schedule or package — we meet as often as feels right for your situation. Some clients book once a month; others come in whenever they hit a specific challenge. You're always in the driver's seat.

**Q: How many sessions will I need?**
A: There's no prescribed number. Some clients resolve a specific issue in one or two sessions; others check in periodically over months or years as life evolves. We'll take it one session at a time and let your progress guide the pace.
