# Redesign Plan — Good Vibrations Health

## The Problem in One Sentence
The current site speaks to Fern's philosophy; the new site must speak to the visitor's problem.

---

## What the Site Is Doing Well (Preserve These)

- **Specific, measurable testimonials** — Pain reduced 8/10 → 0, best sleep in 15 years, neck mass gone in a month. These are extraordinary and must be front and center.
- **Deep, credible credentials** — 25+ years, 15+ certifications, trained by founders of TFT, HADO, and Psych-K, three Joe Dispenza retreats. Currently undersold.
- **Breadth of services** — 10 modalities covering physical pain, anxiety, trauma, habits, and spiritual development.
- **Accessible pricing with flexibility** — $60/$110 is reasonable; "don't let money stop you" is a genuine differentiator.
- **Free entry point** — The complimentary 15-minute consultation is a strong trust-builder.
- **Virtual delivery** — Serves all of Canada. Barely mentioned. Enormous untapped advantage.

---

## Critical Problems — Ranked by Impact

### 1. Homepage speaks to no one's problem
Opening line: *"Thoughts, feelings, and emotions are like waves...arising on the Ocean of Awareness."*
A person Googling "anxiety relief" or "chronic pain help" reads this and immediately leaves. The homepage must open with the visitor's pain, not Fern's philosophy.

### 2. No online booking system
Booking requires: contact form → wait for email → schedule → confirm. Every step is a dropout point. Integrating a real booking tool (Calendly, Acuity, or Jane App) is the single highest-ROI change in the project. The free 15-minute call especially should be self-booking.

### 3. Ten services with no organization causes decision paralysis
HADO, WAVE, PSYCH-K, and Bio-Well mean nothing to a newcomer. No groupings, no hierarchy, no "where do I start?" Services must be organized into outcome-based categories with a guided entry path.

### 4. Pricing is buried on the Contact page
Visitors browse service pages looking for costs and can't find them. Anyone who can't quickly find pricing assumes expensive and leaves. Every service page must show pricing.

### 5. Blog has two posts from 2018
Eight-year-old posts signal to both Google and visitors that the site is abandoned. This devastates SEO and credibility. New posts must be published consistently.

### 6. Credibility risk from some services
Source Energy Medicine (dowsing + coded labels), HADO (written intentions on water bottle), and WAVE (frequency-encoded bottles) will trigger immediate skepticism in new visitors. These services should not be leading, front-facing services — they should appear after trust is established via more familiar modalities.

### 7. The "Gratitude" page has no place in a business nav
Meaningful personally, confusing professionally. Clutters navigation and occupies a top-nav slot. Content belongs in a brief section on the About page.

### 8. URL structure is SEO-incoherent
About is at `/hypnotist/`, Services at `/holistic-therapy-services/`, Testimonials at `/fern-wolf-reviews/`. These slugs confuse structure. Clean up with proper redirects.

### 9. No FAQ and no guided decision path
Common visitor objections ("Is this safe? Will it work for me? Does insurance cover this? What happens in a session?") are buried or absent. An FAQ page converts fence-sitters.

### 10. No video presence, limited photography
Wellness clients invest in a relationship with a practitioner. A 60–90 second welcome video dramatically increases conversion. One photo on the About page is insufficient.

---

## Strategic Direction

**Messaging shift:** Lead with the visitor's problem → earn trust with credentials and testimonials → make booking frictionless.

**Content shift:** Every page answers: What is this? Who is it for? What results can I expect? How does a session work? How much does it cost? How do I book?

**Structure shift:** Replace 10 undifferentiated services with 4 intuitive outcome categories plus condition-based entry points ("I need help with anxiety" → right services listed).

**Conversion shift:** Single dominant CTA throughout (Book Your Free Discovery Call). Online calendar embedded. Pricing visible. Friction removed.

### About Page

**Structure:**
1. **Who I help** — opener about the kinds of people who come to Fern and what brings them. This immediately tells visitors "this is for me."
2. **My approach** — plain-language explanation of holistic, root-cause work vs. symptom management. Philosophy introduced here, after partial trust is built.
3. **My story** — personal arc: what led Fern to this work? (To be filled in with Fern's own narrative)
4. **Background & track record** — 25+ years, based in Toronto, clients across Canada
5. **Credentials grid** — visual display of all 15 certifications. Length of list is a credibility asset.
6. **Training & speaking** — brief section with speaking topics and course info. Link to /training/ for full details.
7. **Gratitude** — short paragraph ("I'm grateful to the teachers who shaped my practice...") with brief list. Right length for a business site.
8. **Video introduction** — 60–90 seconds from Fern, filmed in a warm, well-lit setting. Highest-impact addition.
