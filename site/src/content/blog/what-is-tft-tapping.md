If you've heard the term "tapping" and wondered whether it's legitimate or just another wellness trend, you're not alone. Thought Field Therapy — TFT — gets that reaction a lot. And yet, client after client reports the same thing: pain that's been present for years gone in minutes. Fears that hypnosis and therapy couldn't shift, neutralized in a single session.

So what's actually happening?

## What TFT Is

Thought Field Therapy was developed by psychologist Dr. Roger Callahan in the 1980s after a breakthrough with a patient who had a severe water phobia. He discovered that tapping on specific points on the body — the same meridian points used in Chinese acupuncture — while holding a distressing thought in mind could eliminate the emotional charge behind it rapidly and permanently.

The result wasn't a coping mechanism or a way to manage the feeling. The phobia was gone.

TFT works on the principle that negative emotions, fears, and even physical pain patterns exist as "perturbations" — disruptions in the body's energy field, called the thought field — that are activated when you think about a distressing issue. By applying a specific tapping sequence (called an algorithm) targeted to the type of problem, those perturbations are neutralized. The thought field is cleared. The emotional charge dissolves.

## What a Session Looks Like

There's no talking through trauma. No extended analysis. No re-living difficult experiences in detail.

You bring a problem to mind — a fear, a physical sensation, an emotional memory — and I guide you through a specific sequence of tapping on meridian points on your face, hand, and chest. You rate your distress on a scale of 0 to 10 before and after.

Most people see their number drop significantly — often to zero — within one or two rounds. The whole process takes minutes.

## What It Works For

TFT has proven effective for a wide range of conditions:

- **Anxiety and panic attacks** — often producing immediate, lasting relief
- **Specific phobias** — heights, flying, needles, social situations
- **Trauma and PTSD** — without requiring the client to describe or re-experience events in detail
- **Chronic and acute physical pain** — particularly when there is an emotional component
- **Grief** — reducing the raw emotional charge while preserving the memory
- **Addictive urges** — cravings that arrive suddenly and feel overwhelming
- **Insomnia** — especially when driven by anxious thoughts at bedtime
- **Self-sabotage** — patterns that return even when the person understands them intellectually

## Is It Right for You?

TFT tends to work best when there's a clear, identifiable issue — a specific fear, a recurring physical symptom, a trauma response that gets triggered in particular situations. It's less suited to vague, diffuse dissatisfaction (that's where life coaching tends to shine).

If you've been carrying something that hasn't responded to other approaches, TFT is worth exploring. The first session will tell you a great deal about whether this is the right path for your situation.
